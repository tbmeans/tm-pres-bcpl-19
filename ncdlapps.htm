NC Digital Library app questions

Anticipate that patrons who want an ebook or audiobook on a mobile device will call in with either one of two problems.

  1. Patrons who are not aware of the mobile apps.

  2. Patrons who have the mobile app but can't get it to work for whatever reason.
Patron does not have the app

You can get drawn into a full walkthrough if you tell the patron to begin with the app store. Instead,

  1. check the patron's library card and PIN,
  2. make sure the patron has Wi-Fi access,
  3. direct the patron to the online help for the app,
  4. and tell them to call back if they get stuck.
  • Directing patrons to the online help
  • Google overdrive 2827172 and click the first search result you see.
  • Skip the Libby app recommendation and follow the numbered steps.
Patron does not have the app

I'm recommending the original OverDrive app for beginners and the Libby app for the savvy.

Libby was supposed to make Library-Bookshelf transition more clear than with the OverDrive app Home Menu but there are reports that Libby can be a bit buggy.


Patron does not have the app

If the patron gets stuck or you can't avoid a walkthrough....

  • Ask what kind of mobile device they are using
  • Does the patron seem to not know how to navigate their device?
  • Sign Up & Sign In are the same, just pick one and then touch the "red button"
  • NCDL can be quickly found by searching by Location for Asheville
Patron does not have the app
  • Sign Up & Sign In are the same
  • Touch the red button
  • "Search By" Location and enter "Asheville" to find the NCDL
Patron has app
Can't get it to work
  • Common problems with OverDrive App
  • Error: fulfilled by another user
  • I can't return it
  • Desktop application for audiobooks
  • Solution for fulfillment error is in online help!
  • If they can't return it, they might be trying to return it from the website's Loans bookshelf
    • Any downloaded item has to be returned from the App Bookshelf
    • The App's Home Menu leads you to the App's bookshelf
    • The NCDL's Home Menu is next to the NCDL loans bookshelf icon
  • Good luck with that desktop application for audiobooks....

    FOR PATRONS WHO ALREADY HAVE LIBBY AND LIKE IT...
    if their Libby app has become intractable then either do some form of reset or re-install.

    1. For frozen screens that persist even after closing recently used apps, try each of these in order until one works...

    2. Unlink and relink library card
    3. Reset the libby app
    4. Uninstall and reinstall the Libby App (touch and hold the icon of the app and options will appear)

    These solutions come from The Cape Libraries Automated Materials Sharing website.
